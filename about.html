Our Mission

Connecting people to the help they need

Community Connect is a free, volunteer-powered platform that makes it easy to find, share, and promote local services, programs, and organizations that uplift residents of all backgrounds.

We saw firsthand how many people didn't know about the resources right here in Bothell — food banks, free tutoring, mental health support, and volunteer programs — simply because there was no single place to look. We built that place.

"No one should fall through the cracks just because they didn't know where to look. That's why Community Connect exists."

The Team

Meet the Founders

Community Connect was started by four people who believed local knowledge should be free and accessible to everyone.

AK
Aisha Kimani
Co-Founder & Executive Director

A former social worker with over a decade of experience connecting families to public services. Aisha founded Community Connect after repeatedly seeing clients miss out on critical resources simply because they didn't know where to look.

MO
Marcus Osei
Co-Founder & Tech Lead

Software engineer and civic tech advocate. Marcus built the first version of the platform in a weekend after Aisha's pitch. He believes technology works best when it solves real problems for real people — not just startups.

LP
Lucia Perez
Director of Partnerships

With seven years in the nonprofit sector, Lucia manages relationships with the 48+ organizations currently listed in the hub. She's the reason Community Connect has a trusted reputation among local service providers.

TW
Theo Williams
Community Outreach Lead

Youth advocate and former city council aide. Theo leads outreach across Bothell — from tabling at neighborhood events to running our social media. He grew up in this community and knows its needs better than anyone.

Our Journey

How We Got Here

From a weekend project to a Bothell institution — here's the story of Community Connect.

The Idea Takes Root

Aisha and Marcus meet at a Bothell hackathon and sketch out the first version of Community Connect on a napkin. The goal is simple: one searchable list of every resource in town.

2019
2020

Launch & First 50 Resources

The platform goes live with 50 hand-researched Bothell-area resources. During the pandemic, traffic spikes as residents search for food assistance and health support. We realize we've built something people genuinely need.

Lucia & Theo Join the Team

As word spreads, Lucia and Theo come on board. Lucia lands our first formal nonprofit partnerships. Theo begins representing Community Connect at community meetings and city events.

2021
2022

100+ Resources & First Events

The directory grows past 100 verified resources. We host our first Community Resource Fair, bringing together 30+ organizations and 800+ attendees in a single afternoon.

Volunteer Network Expands

Over 400 community volunteers sign up to help verify listings, run workshops, and support events. The platform becomes genuinely community-run — not just community-serving.

2023
2024

500+ Resources & 48 Partners

We hit 500 verified resources and 48 organizational partners. The platform now sees 12,000+ monthly visitors. We launch the Spotlight section to highlight standout organizations making the biggest impact.
